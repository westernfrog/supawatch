## Design Context

### Users
Public-facing audience of general movie/TV fans — not just the builder. Visitors arrive with no prior context on the project, so the interface needs to read as a polished, intentional product on first impression, across phone and desktop, in typically low-light "what should I watch tonight" browsing sessions.

### Brand Personality
Cinematic, brutalist/raw. The interface should feel directed — like a film set, with dramatic light/dark contrast and deliberate motion — while staying stark and unadorned: sharp edges over rounded comfort, confident negative space, no decorative softness for its own sake.

### Aesthetic Direction
Dark-first (`#010101` background), minimal brutalist layout with strong typographic hierarchy as the primary design tool. Cinematic transitions (cross-dissolve backdrops, blur-fade-ins, ken-burns) carry the "directed" feeling; UI chrome itself stays quiet and gets out of the way of posters/stills. Brand red (`#e50914`) is a rare, deliberate accent — not a wash. Avoid reading as a generic Netflix/Prime clone; avoid generic AI-slop defaults (glassmorphism, gradient text, side-stripe cards, bento grids, sparkline decoration). Fonts: MDNichrome (display/logo), Ronzino (`--font-manrope`, body), IBM Plex Mono + Space Grotesk (labels/mono accents). No rounded-corner icon-above-heading pattern; sharp corners throughout (no `rounded-*` on cards/buttons).

### Design Principles
1. **Content is the decoration.** Posters, stills, and backdrops carry the visual weight; UI elements stay quiet, sharp-edged, and out of the way.
2. **Motion is directed, not decorative.** Reserve elaborate motion (cinematic dissolves, blur-reveals) for genuine state/scene changes; everyday interactions (hover, filter toggles) get fast, restrained feedback only.
3. **Red is a flare, not a wash.** Brand red appears as small, deliberate marks (accent bars, active states, HAL's eye) — never as page-wide gradients or ambient glows.
4. **Typography does the hierarchy work.** Size/weight contrast over boxes, borders, or color-coding to separate sections.
5. **Public-facing polish.** Every state (loading, empty, error, focus, touch target) needs to hold up for a stranger's first visit, not just the builder's own use.

### Quality Bar
Flagship for this polish pass on `/search` — sweat alignment, motion, contrast, and edge cases in full.
